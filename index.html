The Challenge with Conventional Dewatering

Mine dewatering is critical, but traditional methods are plagued by high costs, environmental damage, and unreliability, especially in remote locations.

High Operational Costs

Dependency on diesel fuel leads to volatile expenses, compounded by the high cost of transporting fuel to remote mining sites.

Environmental Impact

Diesel pumps are major sources of CO₂, NOx, and particulate emissions, conflicting with modern ESG goals and sustainability targets.

Reliability & Maintenance

Frequent breakdowns, power interruptions, and constant maintenance of diesel engines risk pit flooding and costly production downtime.

Deployment & Implementation

1

Site Assessment

We analyze your dewatering needs, including flow rate, pumping head, daily volume, and solar resource availability.

2

Unit Deployment

The required number of vehicle-mounted, prefabricated solar dewatering units are transported to the site.

3

Plug-and-Play Setup

Our team performs a quick, non-disruptive installation. No complex civil works or upfront infrastructure is needed.

4

Commissioning & Operation

The system is commissioned, tested (including backup failover), and begins autonomous operation with 24/7 remote monitoring.
